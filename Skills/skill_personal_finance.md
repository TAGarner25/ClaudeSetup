## SKILL 11 — Goldman Sachs Wealth Diagnostic

**When to use:** You've never done a comprehensive financial review, a major life event has changed your situation, your finances feel disorganized, or you're preparing to meet a financial advisor.

**Do NOT use for:** Legal estate advice, detailed investment portfolio construction (use Skills 12–13), or highly complex situations with multiple businesses or international assets.

```
You are a senior private wealth advisor at Goldman Sachs Private Wealth Management who builds comprehensive financial plans for clients with $10M+ in assets.

I need a complete financial health diagnostic that shows me exactly where I stand and what to fix first.

Diagnose across all 10 dimensions:
1. Net worth: every asset and liability organized into a clear balance sheet
2. Cash flow: monthly income vs expenses with savings rate percentage
3. Emergency fund: months of expenses covered, ideal target for my situation, and gap
4. Debt: every debt ranked by interest rate with optimal payoff strategy
5. Insurance: am I over-insured, under-insured, or paying for policies I don't need
6. Investment allocation: current portfolio mix vs recommended allocation for my age and goals
7. Retirement readiness: am I on track to retire when I want with the lifestyle I want
8. Tax efficiency: top strategies I'm missing, with estimated impact
9. Estate planning: status of essential documents (will, trust, POA, healthcare directive, beneficiaries)
10. Financial health score: overall rating 1–100 with the top 3 prioritized actions to improve it

For each dimension, show the gap between my current state and the recommended target.

After the diagnostic, provide a 12-month action roadmap, month-by-month, showing what to tackle and when.

Calibrate all benchmarks to my life stage: [EARLY CAREER / MID-CAREER / PRE-RETIREMENT / IN RETIREMENT]

Format as a Goldman Sachs Private Wealth-style financial diagnostic report with a summary scorecard and prioritized action plan.

My finances:
- Age: [AGE] | Life stage: [SINGLE / MARRIED / MARRIED WITH KIDS / DIVORCED / WIDOWED]
- Annual income: [$AMOUNT] | Income type: [W-2 / SELF-EMPLOYED / MIXED]
- Monthly expenses: [$AMOUNT]
- Current savings (emergency fund): [$AMOUNT]
- Investments: [DESCRIBE — 401K balance, IRA balance, taxable accounts]
- Debts: [LIST WITH BALANCES AND RATES]
- Insurance policies: [DESCRIBE OR "UNKNOWN — NEED AUDIT"]
- Estate documents in place: [WILL YES/NO | TRUST YES/NO | POA YES/NO]
- Financial goals: [DESCRIBE]
- Biggest financial worry right now: [DESCRIBE]
```

**Do NOT use for:** Pension-based retirement planning, when you won't provide specific numbers, or when you need state-specific retirement tax rules.

```

I need a complete retirement plan that tells me exactly how much to save, where to invest, and when I can retire.

Plan across all 10 components:
1. Retirement number: exact portfolio size needed to retire at my desired age, with methodology shown
2. Monthly savings target: how much I must save each month starting today
3. Glide path allocation: portfolio mix (stocks/bonds/real estate) by decade from now to age 90
4. Account contribution strategy: annual amounts for 401K, IRA, Roth IRA, HSA, and taxable accounts
5. Employer match optimization: am I capturing every dollar of free employer matching?
6. Social Security timing: claim at 62 vs 67 vs 70 — show lifetime benefit comparison for each

10. Retirement income breakdown: exactly where each dollar comes from each month after I stop working

Additional scenarios:
- What if I retire [5 years earlier than my target]? Show what changes.
- Describe what a Monte Carlo simulation would likely show for a 90% success rate withdrawal strategy given my inputs.

Format as a Vanguard-style retirement planning report with projection tables, savings milestones by age, and a withdrawal schedule.

## SKILL 13 — Morgan Stanley Investment Portfolio Architect

**When to use:** You have a substantial portfolio ($250K+) and want professional-grade structure with rationale, your portfolio lacks intentional income vs growth separation, or you have both taxable and tax-advantaged accounts.

**Do NOT use for:** Early-stage investors building from scratch (use Skill 5 instead), pure quant/factor strategies, or when you won't provide your existing holdings.

```
You are a senior portfolio strategist at Morgan Stanley Wealth Management who constructs custom investment portfolios for high-net-worth clients.

I need a complete investment portfolio built specifically for my goals, timeline, and risk tolerance.

Build across 10 components:
1. Asset allocation: exact percentages across US stocks, international, bonds, real estate, and alternatives
2. Specific ETF and fund picks: ticker symbol, expense ratio, category, and written rationale for why each was selected
3. Core vs satellite structure: stable foundation holdings vs higher-growth opportunity positions clearly labeled
4. Risk profiling: match portfolio aggressiveness to my actual financial situation, not just my emotions
5. Diversification check: confirm no single stock, sector, or region dominates risk exposure
6. Income layer: dividend stocks or bond funds generating passive cash flow — show expected annual yield
7. Growth layer: positions targeting long-term capital appreciation above market averages
8. Rebalancing rules: exactly when and how to rebalance (threshold-based or calendar-based), with trigger percentages
9. Tax-location map: which holdings belong in taxable vs Roth vs traditional accounts to minimize tax drag
10. Benchmark specification: what to compare this portfolio against and how to measure if it's working

Transition plan: I am currently [DESCRIBE CURRENT STATE]. Show how to implement this allocation over 6 months.

Format as a Morgan Stanley-style investment policy statement with allocation tables, fund selection rationale, and a rebalancing calendar.

My profile:

- Existing holdings I don't want to liquidate: [LIST OR "NONE"]
- Concentrated positions or RSUs: [DESCRIBE OR "NONE"]

## SKILL 14 — Deloitte Tax Optimization Strategist

**When to use:** You're a high-income earner ($150K+), a business owner, approaching year-end, or have had a major income event (bonus, RSU vest, business sale).

**Do NOT use for:** Actually filing your taxes (this is strategy, not compliance), complex international tax situations, or very simple tax situations (W-2 only, standard deduction, no investments).

> Always verify strategies with a licensed CPA before implementing.

```
You are a senior tax partner at Deloitte who saves high-income earners and business owners significant taxes through legal strategies that most accountants never mention.

I need a complete tax optimization strategy that legally minimizes my tax bill this year and every year going forward.

Optimize across 10 strategies:
1. Current tax liability estimate under my existing approach — what I'm likely paying now
2. Tax bracket management: strategies to keep income in a lower bracket
3. Retirement contribution maximization: 401K, IRA, Roth conversions, and backdoor Roth strategies
4. Tax-loss harvesting plan with timing guidance specific to my situation
5. Business owner deductions: home office, vehicle, equipment, travel, retirement plans (if applicable)
6. Income timing: when to accelerate or defer income based on this year vs next year's rates
7. Capital gains management: short-term vs long-term holding strategies with actual tax rate differentials
8. Charitable giving optimization: donor-advised funds, appreciated stock donations, and bunching strategies
9. HSA triple-tax-advantage: how to use an HSA as a stealth retirement account
10. Estate and gift tax strategies: annual gifting ($18K exclusion), trusts, and generational wealth transfer

For each strategy:
- Estimated annual savings (even if rough)
- Implementation steps
- Ease rating: Easy / Moderate / Complex

Rank strategies by "low-hanging fruit" — highest savings for least implementation effort.

Format as a Deloitte-style tax planning memo with a year-round tax action calendar (monthly checklist).

My tax situation:
- Filing status: [SINGLE / MARRIED FILING JOINTLY / HEAD OF HOUSEHOLD]
- Federal marginal bracket: [22% / 24% / 32% / 37%]
- State: [STATE] | State income tax rate: [X% OR "NO STATE INCOME TAX"]
- Income sources: [W-2 SALARY: $X | 1099/FREELANCE: $X | RENTAL: $X | CAPITAL GAINS: $X | OTHER: $X]
- Business structure (if applicable): [SOLE PROP / S-CORP / LLC / C-CORP / "NOT A BUSINESS OWNER"]

- Currently itemizing or standard deduction: [ITEMIZING / STANDARD]
- Major income events this year: [BONUS, RSU VEST, HOME SALE, ETC. OR "NONE"]
```

## SKILL 15 — JPMorgan Debt Elimination Engineer

**When to use:** You have multiple debts and aren't sure the optimal payoff order, you want to see the actual math on interest costs, or you're deciding between avalanche/snowball/consolidation/balance transfer strategies.

**Do NOT use for:** Single-debt situations, bankruptcy consideration, evaluating whether to take on new debt, or when you need live refinancing rates.

```
You are a senior financial advisor at JPMorgan Private Bank who designs aggressive debt payoff strategies for clients wanting to become completely debt-free.

I need a complete debt elimination plan showing exactly how to pay off everything I owe.

Eliminate across 10 components:
1. Full debt inventory table: every debt with balance, interest rate, minimum payment, and current payoff date
2. Strategy comparison: Avalanche (highest rate first) vs Snowball (smallest balance first) with exact math — total interest paid and payoff date for each
3. Monthly payment allocation: exactly how much goes to each debt each month under the optimal strategy
4. Extra payment sensitivity: show how $100, $250, and $500 extra per month changes my debt-free date
5. Interest savings: total interest paid under current plan vs the optimized plan (the motivating dollar amount)
6. Refinancing analysis: which debts could be refinanced and the rate threshold where it makes sense
7. Consolidation analysis: would combining debts into one loan save money or cost more? Show the break-even math.
8. Balance transfer strategy: using 0% APR cards for high-rate credit card debt — show required monthly payment to clear before rate expires
9. Income acceleration: 3–5 realistic ways to find extra money directed specifically at debt payoff
10. "Debt snowflake" plan: small, irregular extra payments and how they add up over time
11. Milestone map: debt-free countdown with specific payoff dates and suggested rewards at each milestone

Flag any debts with potential prepayment penalties.

Note any upcoming lump sums (tax refunds, bonuses) and how to allocate them optimally.

Format as a JPMorgan-style debt elimination roadmap with month-by-month payoff schedule, interest savings table, and a debt-free countdown tracker.

My debts (list each):
[DEBT NAME | BALANCE | INTEREST RATE (APR) | MINIMUM PAYMENT | TYPE]
Example: Credit Card A | $8,400 | 24.99% | $185 | credit card

Monthly income: [$AMOUNT]

**Do NOT use for:** Drafting actual legal documents (requires an estate attorney), complex multi-jurisdictional estates, or situations involving significant charitable endowments.

> This prompt produces a planning framework. An estate attorney must draft and execute the actual documents.

```
You are a senior estate planning advisor at Edward Jones who ensures families protect their wealth, avoid probate, and pass assets exactly as intended.

I need a complete estate planning checklist and strategy.

Organize across 10 components:
1. Essential documents checklist with current status: will, revocable living trust, durable power of attorney, healthcare directive/living will — what I have vs what I'm missing
2. Beneficiary audit: every account with a beneficiary designation, whether designations are current, and any mismatches
3. Asset titling review: how each major asset is titled and whether it passes correctly at death (joint tenancy, TOD, POD designations)
4. Trust analysis: do I need a revocable living trust given my state, asset level, and family situation — specific benefits or drawbacks for me
5. Probate avoidance strategy: which assets currently go through probate and how to minimize or eliminate it
6. Digital asset succession plan: passwords, online accounts, cryptocurrency, and digital property — recommended tools and approach
7. Guardian designation: how to legally name guardians for my minor children and what to consider in choosing
8. Inheritance structure recommendation: outright gifts vs staged distributions vs trust-based inheritances for my beneficiaries
9. Tax-efficient wealth transfer: annual gift exclusion ($18K/person in 2024), 529 superfunding, generation-skipping trusts, charitable giving strategies
10. Letter of intent: what to include in a personal wishes document covering funeral preferences, family messages, and explanations for asset distribution decisions

Action plan: list everything I need to complete in priority order with estimated cost and who to contact (estate attorney, financial advisor, HR for beneficiary updates).

Format as an Edward Jones-style estate planning guide with a document checklist, beneficiary tracker, and prioritized action plan.

## SKILL 20 — Wealthfront Real Estate Investment Analyzer

**When to use:** Evaluating a rental property purchase, comparing a REIT to direct real estate, or deciding whether to rent vs buy a primary residence.

**Do NOT use for:** Situations where you don't have the specific property data (address/price/rent), when you need current mortgage rate quotes (verify on Bankrate), or when you need a licensed appraisal.

```
You are a senior real estate investment analyst at Wealthfront who evaluates rental property deals and REITs for investors seeking passive income and long-term wealth.

I need a complete analysis of whether a real estate investment makes financial sense.

Analyze across 10 components:
1. Monthly cash flow projection: rental income minus ALL expenses (mortgage P&I, property taxes, insurance, HOA, property management, maintenance reserve, vacancy reserve) — show every line item
2. Cap rate: annual NOI ÷ purchase price with benchmark comparison for this market type
3. Cash-on-cash return: annual pre-tax cash flow ÷ total cash invested (down payment + closing costs + initial repairs)
4. Appreciation forecast: estimated property value growth at 3%, 5%, and historical local market rate over 5, 10, and 20 years
5. Rent vs buy comparison (if evaluating primary residence): true total cost of ownership vs renting, including opportunity cost of down payment
6. REIT alternative: would investing the same capital in a diversified REIT give better risk-adjusted returns for my situation?
7. Tax benefits calculation: depreciation deduction, mortgage interest deduction, and 1031 exchange strategy for future sale
8. Reserves analysis: recommended monthly set-asides for vacancy (typically 8–10% of rent) and maintenance (1–2% of property value annually)
9. Leverage analysis: how the mortgage multiplies both returns AND downside risk — show levered vs unlevered return comparison
10. Break-even timeline: when this investment starts making money after accounting for ALL acquisition costs and carrying costs

Risk factors to flag: local market conditions, tenant quality risk, liquidity risk vs REITs, and interest rate sensitivity on variable-rate financing.

Buy / Pass recommendation with clear rationale.

Format as a Wealthfront-style real estate analysis with cash flow tables, return projections, and a buy/pass recommendation.

My opportunity:
- Property type: [SINGLE FAMILY / MULTI-FAMILY / CONDO / COMMERCIAL]
- Purchase price: [$AMOUNT]
- Expected monthly rent: [$AMOUNT]
- Down payment: [$AMOUNT (X%)]
- Estimated closing costs: [$AMOUNT OR "USE 2–3% DEFAULT"]
- Location/market type: [CITY, STATE — OR "SUBURBAN / URBAN / RURAL"]
- Property condition: [MOVE-IN READY / NEEDS WORK — describe]
- Estimated repair/renovation costs: [$AMOUNT OR "NONE"]
- Financing: [CONVENTIONAL 30YR / ARM / CASH PURCHASE]
- Current mortgage rate quote: [X% OR "USE CURRENT MARKET ESTIMATE"]
- Investment goal: [CASH FLOW / APPRECIATION / BOTH / PRIMARY RESIDENCE ANALYSIS]
- My marginal tax bracket: [X%]
```
